## Definition

Type 1 diabetes (T1D) results from autoimmune destruction of pancreatic β-cells, causing absolute insulin deficiency. It accounts for 5–10% of all diabetes cases. Onset occurs at any age, though historically associated with childhood and adolescence.

## Staging

| Stage | Autoimmunity | Glycemia | Symptoms |
|-------|-------------|----------|----------|
| 1 | ≥2 islet autoantibodies | Normoglycemia | None |
| 2 | ≥1 islet autoantibody | Dysglycemia (IFG/IGT/A1C 5.7–6.4%) | None |
| 3 | Autoantibodies may disappear | Overt diabetes by standard criteria | Present |

At Stage 1: ~44% 5-year risk of progressing to overt T1D. At Stage 2: ~60% within 2 years, ~75% within 5 years.

## Key Autoantibodies

- Glutamic acid decarboxylase (GAD65) — primary antibody to test first
- Islet antigen 2 (IA-2) and IA-2β
- Zinc transporter 8 (ZnT8)
- Insulin autoantibody (IAA) — useful before insulin treatment

A single IA-2 autoantibody warrants monitoring equivalent to multiple autoantibody positivity.

## AABBCC Differentiation Tool

Helps distinguish T1D from T2D:
- **A**ge <35 years → consider T1D
- **A**utoimmunity (personal/family history)
- **B**ody habitus BMI <25 kg/m²
- **B**ackground (family history of T1D)
- **C**ontrol failure on non-insulin therapies
- **C**omorbidities (e.g., immune checkpoint inhibitor use)

## Special Considerations

- **LADA (Latent Autoimmune Diabetes in Adults):** Slowly progressive autoimmune T1D in adults; acceptable term clinically. Raises awareness of slow β-cell destruction in adults.
- **ICI-induced diabetes:** Occurs in 0.6–1.4% of people on immune checkpoint inhibitors; fulminant onset with DKA; fewer than half have islet autoantibodies.
- **Teplizumab:** FDA-approved to delay Stage 2 → Stage 3 progression. Not indicated for established Stage 3.
- Misdiagnosis rate up to 40% in adults with new T1D (often labeled T2D).

## Associated Autoimmune Conditions

T1D co-occurs with: Hashimoto thyroiditis, Graves disease, celiac disease, Addison disease, vitiligo, pernicious anemia, autoimmune hepatitis.
