## Diabetes Diagnostic Criteria (Any One Sufficient)

| Criterion | Threshold |
|-----------|-----------|
| A1C | ≥6.5% (≥48 mmol/mol) |
| Fasting Plasma Glucose (FPG) | ≥126 mg/dL (≥7.0 mmol/L); fasting = no caloric intake ≥8 h |
| 2-h Plasma Glucose (2-h PG) during 75-g OGTT | ≥200 mg/dL (≥11.1 mmol/L) |
| Random Plasma Glucose + Classic Symptoms | ≥200 mg/dL (≥11.1 mmol/L) with polyuria, polydipsia, or unexplained weight loss |

## Confirmation Requirement

In the **absence of unequivocal hyperglycemia**, diagnosis requires **two abnormal results** — either:
- Same test repeated on a different day, OR
- Two different tests (e.g., A1C and FPG) obtained simultaneously or on different days

If two tests are discordant, repeat the one above the threshold.

## A1C Considerations

- Must be performed with NGSP-certified, DCCT-traceable method
- Reflects weighted average of glucose over ~120 days (more sensitive to recent glucose)
- **Not suitable** when: hemoglobin variants (sickle cell, HbEE), anemia, erythropoietin use, hemodialysis, HIV, pregnancy, G6PD deficiency → use plasma glucose instead
- X-linked G6PD G202A variant (carried by ~11% of Black individuals) lowers A1C by ~0.7–0.8%; use glucose criteria
- Controversy around modest racial differences (~0.3% higher A1C in Black individuals) — do not use race as proxy for genetic A1C variation

## Point-of-Care A1C

For screening/diagnosis: restricted to FDA-approved devices at CLIA-certified labs with trained personnel and proficiency testing.

## OGTT Preparation

Participants must consume ≥150 g carbohydrates/day for 3 days prior to OGTT. Carbohydrate restriction beforehand causes false elevation of post-challenge glucose.

## Discordant Results

Consistent substantial discordance between glucose and A1C should prompt investigation for analytical interference. Consider fructosamine or glycated albumin as alternative chronic glycemia markers.
