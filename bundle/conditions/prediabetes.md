## Diagnostic Criteria (Any One Sufficient)

| Test | Prediabetes Range |
|------|------------------|
| A1C | 5.7–6.4% (39–47 mmol/mol) |
| Fasting Plasma Glucose (FPG) | 100–125 mg/dL (5.6–6.9 mmol/L) — Impaired Fasting Glucose (IFG) |
| 2-h PG during 75-g OGTT | 140–199 mg/dL (7.8–11.0 mmol/L) — Impaired Glucose Tolerance (IGT) |

Risk is continuous: rises with higher values and is greatest at the upper end of each range.

## Clinical Significance

- Strong risk factor for progression to T2D and for cardiovascular disease
- Associated with obesity (especially abdominal), dyslipidemia (high TG/low HDL), and hypertension
- 5-year risk of progressing to T2D: 9–25% for A1C 5.5–6.0%; 25–50% for A1C 6.0–6.5%
- Aggressive intervention warranted for those with A1C >6.0% or combined IFG+IGT

## Intervention Targets

- **Goal:** ≥5–7% weight loss from baseline to prevent progression
- Intensive lifestyle intervention (150+ min/week moderate physical activity + dietary changes) is primary prevention
- Metformin may be considered when lifestyle alone is insufficient (especially BMI ≥35, younger adults, or A1C >6.0%)
- Prevention benefit mainly demonstrated for IGT ± elevated fasting glucose, less so for isolated IFG or A1C-defined prediabetes alone

## Monitoring After Diagnosis

- Annual repeat testing if known prediabetes
- Every 1–3 years if prior gestational diabetes
- Minimum 3-year intervals if normal screen, sooner with symptom onset or weight gain

## Note on OGTT vs. FPG vs. A1C

The 2-h OGTT identifies more people with prediabetes/diabetes than FPG or A1C at defined cut points and is the most sensitive screen. FPG/A1C preferred in routine practice for convenience.
