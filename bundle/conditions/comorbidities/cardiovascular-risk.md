## Overview

Cardiovascular disease (CVD) — including ASCVD and heart failure — is the leading cause of death in diabetes. All major CV risk factors cluster in diabetes. Comprehensive multifactorial risk reduction is essential.

## Blood Pressure

**Goal:** <130/80 mmHg for most; <120 mmHg systolic encouraged in those with high CV or kidney risk (supported by BPROAD and ESPRIT trials).

**Pharmacologic approach:**
- BP ≥130/80 → initiate medication + lifestyle
- BP ≥150/90 → start with two drugs or single-pill combination
- **First-line for albuminuria or coronary artery disease:** ACE inhibitor or ARB
- Other accepted first-line agents: thiazide-like diuretics, dihydropyridine calcium channel blockers
- β-Blockers: only indicated for prior MI, active angina, or HFrEF
- Do NOT combine ACE inhibitor + ARB (or add direct renin inhibitor)
- Resistant hypertension (on 3 drugs including diuretic): add mineralocorticoid receptor antagonist (MRA)

**Lifestyle for BP:** Weight loss, DASH eating pattern, sodium <2,300 mg/day, limit alcohol, ≥150 min/week aerobic activity, smoking cessation.

**Monitor:** eGFR and potassium after starting ACE-i/ARB/MRA; potassium 7–14 days after diuretic initiation.

## Lipid Management

**Screening:** Lipid panel at diagnosis, initial medical evaluation, then as indicated.

### LDL Cholesterol Targets

| Population | Target | Therapy |
|-----------|--------|---------|
| Ages 40–75, no ASCVD | <70 mg/dL if high CV risk | Moderate-intensity statin |
| Ages 40–75, high CV risk or multiple risk factors | <70 mg/dL (≥50% reduction) | High-intensity statin |
| Established ASCVD | <55 mg/dL (≥50% reduction) | High-intensity statin + ezetimibe or PCSK9i if needed |
| Ages 20–39 with ASCVD risk factors | Consider moderate statin | Discuss benefit/risk |
| Ages >75, already on statin | Continue | — |

**Statin dosing:**
- High-intensity: atorvastatin 40–80 mg, rosuvastatin 20–40 mg
- Moderate: atorvastatin 10–20 mg, rosuvastatin 5–10 mg, simvastatin 20–40 mg

**Statin intolerance alternatives:** bempedoic acid (reduces 4-point MACE), PCSK9 monoclonal antibodies (alirocumab, evolocumab).

**Note:** Statins modestly increase T2D risk (~9% odds ratio); the cardiovascular benefit far outweighs this risk.

**Do not combine statin + fibrate (no added ASCVD benefit, increased myopathy risk).**
**Do not combine statin + niacin (no benefit, increased adverse effects).**

### Triglycerides

- TG ≥500 mg/dL: treat to prevent pancreatitis (fibrate, fish oil, very low fat diet)
- TG 150–499 mg/dL + established CVD on statin: consider adding icosapent ethyl (REDUCE-IT: 25% relative risk reduction)

## Antiplatelet Therapy

| Setting | Recommendation |
|---------|---------------|
| Secondary prevention (established ASCVD) | Aspirin 75–162 mg/day (strongly recommended) |
| Aspirin allergy + ASCVD | Clopidogrel 75 mg/day |
| After ACS/coronary stenting | Dual antiplatelet (aspirin + P2Y12 inhibitor); duration determined by interprofessional team |
| Stable coronary/PAD, low bleeding risk | Consider aspirin 81 mg + rivaroxaban 2.5 mg BID (COMPASS trial) |
| Primary prevention | Consider in age ≥50 + ≥1 major CV risk factor + low bleeding risk; weigh benefit vs. bleeding risk |

## Cardioprotective Medications (Beyond Glycemic Effect)

### SGLT2 Inhibitors — Indicated when:
- Established ASCVD or high CV risk: canagliflozin, empagliflozin for MACE reduction
- Heart failure (preserved or reduced EF): empagliflozin, dapagliflozin for HF hospitalization reduction
- CKD with eGFR 20–60 or albuminuria: kidney and CV protection

### GLP-1 Receptor Agonists — Indicated when:
- Established ASCVD: dulaglutide, liraglutide, semaglutide (SC and oral) reduce MACE
- CKD: semaglutide for albuminuria/kidney endpoints
- Obesity with HFpEF: semaglutide reduces HF symptoms; tirzepatide reduces HF events

### Combined SGLT2i + GLP-1 RA
May be considered for additive CV and kidney risk reduction in T2D with ASCVD/CKD.

## Heart Failure Screening

- Measure natriuretic peptide (BNP or NT-proBNP) in adults with diabetes to screen for Stage B HF
- If elevated: echocardiography to identify structural abnormalities

## CV Screening

- Routine coronary artery disease screening NOT recommended in asymptomatic individuals
- Investigate CAD with symptoms, carotid bruits, TIA/stroke, claudication, PAD, or ECG abnormalities
- PAD screening with ankle-brachial index in adults ≥65 years, microvascular disease, foot complications, or end-organ damage if result would change management
