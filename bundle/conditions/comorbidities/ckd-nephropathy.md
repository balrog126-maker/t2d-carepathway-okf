## Overview

Diabetic kidney disease (DKD) is the leading cause of chronic kidney disease (CKD) and end-stage kidney disease (ESKD) globally. It affects ~40% of people with T1D and T2D and substantially increases cardiovascular risk. Early detection and targeted treatment can slow progression.

## Diagnostic Criteria

### eGFR Staging (GFR Categories)

| Stage | eGFR (mL/min/1.73 m²) | Description |
|-------|----------------------|-------------|
| G1 | ≥90 | Normal or high (CKD if albuminuria or structural abnormality present) |
| G2 | 60–89 | Mildly decreased |
| G3a | 45–59 | Mildly to moderately decreased |
| G3b | 30–44 | Moderately to severely decreased |
| G4 | 15–29 | Severely decreased |
| G5 | <15 | Kidney failure (ESKD) |

### UACR (Albuminuria) Staging

| Category | UACR (mg/g creatinine) | Interpretation |
|----------|------------------------|----------------|
| A1 | <30 | Normal to mildly increased |
| A2 | 30–300 | Moderately increased (formerly "microalbuminuria") |
| A3 | >300 | Severely increased (formerly "macroalbuminuria") |

CKD is defined as eGFR <60 or UACR ≥30 persisting for >3 months on at least 2 separate tests.

### Prognosis Grid

Risk of CKD progression (low / moderate / high / very high) is determined by the intersection of GFR stage and albuminuria category. G3b–G4 + A3 = very high risk.

## Pathophysiology

- Hemodynamic: glomerular hypertension → hyperfiltration → glomerular damage
- Metabolic: AGE accumulation, oxidative stress, inflammation from chronic hyperglycemia
- Renin-angiotensin-aldosterone system (RAAS) overactivation
- Progression: normoalbuminuria → A2 → A3 → declining eGFR → ESKD

## Monitoring

| Test | Frequency |
|------|-----------|
| Spot urine albumin-to-creatinine ratio (UACR) | At least annually in all people with diabetes |
| Serum creatinine + eGFR (CKD-EPI equation) | At least annually |
| Serum potassium | At least annually; more frequently if on ACE-i/ARB/MRA or hyperkalemia history |
| eGFR + UACR | Every 3–6 months for G3b+ or rapidly declining eGFR |

False-positive UACR: heavy exercise within 24h, urinary tract infection, fever, acute illness — repeat before confirming CKD.

## Blood Pressure Target

- <130/80 mmHg for all people with diabetes + CKD
- ACE inhibitor or ARB is first-line when UACR ≥30 (reduces albuminuria and slows CKD progression independent of BP lowering)
- Do NOT combine ACE-i + ARB

## Medication Implications by eGFR

### Metformin
| eGFR | Action |
|------|--------|
| ≥45 | Continue; no dose adjustment |
| 30–44 | Reduce dose (max ≤1,000 mg/day); monitor every 3–6 months |
| <30 | Contraindicated |

### SGLT2 Inhibitors
| eGFR | Action |
|------|--------|
| ≥45 | Full glycemic + organ-protective dose |
| 25–44 | Reduced glycemic efficacy; continue for renal/CV protection (empagliflozin, dapagliflozin) |
| 20–<25 | May continue dapagliflozin/empagliflozin for organ protection per trial data |
| <20 | Insufficient data; generally avoid |

SGLT2 inhibitors with demonstrated renal protection: **canagliflozin** (CREDENCE), **dapagliflozin** (DAPA-CKD), **empagliflozin** (EMPA-KIDNEY).

### GLP-1 Receptor Agonists
- Generally safe across CKD stages; no dose adjustment needed based on eGFR alone
- Semaglutide (FLOW trial): significantly reduced kidney composite endpoint in T2D + CKD
- Preferred alternative when SGLT2i not tolerated or contraindicated
- Use with caution in severe gastroparesis (reduced absorption of oral agents)

### Insulin
- Insulin clearance decreases as eGFR declines → increased hypoglycemia risk
- Dose reduction often needed in CKD G3b–G5
- Monitor glucose more frequently; raise A1C target if hypoglycemia is recurrent

### Other Agents
| Agent | CKD Guidance |
|-------|-------------|
| ACE-i / ARB | First-line for UACR ≥30; monitor potassium and creatinine after initiation |
| MRA (finerenone) | Reduces CKD progression and CV events in T2D + CKD (FIDELIO-DKD, FIGARO-DKD); requires eGFR ≥25 and normokalemia |
| DPP-4 inhibitors | Dose adjustment for most agents except linagliptin (hepatically cleared) |
| Sulfonylureas | Significant hypoglycemia risk in CKD; glipizide safest if needed |

## Referral to Nephrology

Refer when:
- eGFR <30 (G4) for preparation for renal replacement therapy
- eGFR decline >5 mL/min/year
- UACR >300 with uncertain etiology
- Difficult-to-manage hyperkalemia or hypertension
- Suspected non-diabetic kidney disease (rapid decline, active urine sediment, no albuminuria)
