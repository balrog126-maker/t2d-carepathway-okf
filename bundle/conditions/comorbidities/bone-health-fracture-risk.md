## Key Concept

Both T1D and T2D independently increase fracture risk beyond what bone mineral density (BMD) predicts. Standard tools (FRAX, T-score) underestimate fracture risk in diabetes.

## Risk Magnitude

| Diabetes Type | Hip Fracture Risk vs. No Diabetes |
|--------------|----------------------------------|
| T1D | 4.35× increased |
| T2D | 1.79× increased (despite normal/higher BMD) |

Overall 34% higher fracture risk compared to people without diabetes.

## Why BMD Underestimates Risk in Diabetes

- T2D patients have 5–10% **higher** BMD than controls, yet fracture more
- Advanced glycation end products (AGEs) accumulate in bone matrix
- Microvascular damage and proinflammatory state impair bone repair
- Poor physical function, neuropathy, retinopathy, and hypoglycemia → falls

## FRAX Adjustment for T2D

Standard FRAX underestimates risk. Proposed corrections:
- Use rheumatoid arthritis input as a T2D surrogate
- Lower femoral neck T-score input by 0.5 SD (e.g., interpret −2.0 as −2.5)
- Add 10 years to stated age

## BMD Screening Recommendations

Perform DXA scan in:
- Adults ≥65 years
- Postmenopausal women and men ≥50 years with any of:
  - Frequent hypoglycemic events
  - Diabetes duration >10 years
  - Insulin, thiazolidinedione, or sulfonylurea use
  - A1C >8%
  - Peripheral/autonomic neuropathy, retinopathy, or nephropathy
  - Frequent falls or glucocorticoid use

Reassess every 2–3 years.

## Pharmacologic Treatment Thresholds

- T-score ≤−2.5 → consider pharmacologic therapy (same as general population)
- T2D with T-score −2.0 to −2.5 + additional risk factors → also consider treatment
- First-line: alendronate, risedronate, ibandronate, zoledronate, or denosumab

## Glucose-Lowering Medications and Bone Safety

| Medication | Bone Impact |
|-----------|-------------|
| Metformin | Safe/neutral |
| Thiazolidinediones (TZD) | Doubles fracture risk in women; avoid in high-risk individuals |
| Sulfonylureas | Modestly increased risk via hypoglycemia → falls |
| GLP-1 RAs | Likely neutral to beneficial at low doses |
| SGLT2 inhibitors | Mixed; canagliflozin showed some fracture signal; empagliflozin/dapagliflozin neutral |
| Insulin | Higher risk, likely via hypoglycemia and longer disease duration |

## Key Management Points

- Minimize hypoglycemia to prevent falls
- Promote moderate physical activity (aerobic + weight-bearing + balance)
- Calcium 1,000–1,200 mg/day, vitamin D to maintain 25-OH-vit D ≥20 ng/mL
- After fragility fracture: start antiosteoporosis therapy immediately; diagnose osteoporosis regardless of BMD
