## Overview

People with T1D carry elevated risk for other organ-specific autoimmune conditions. These occur more frequently than in the general population and may be clinically silent at onset.

## Common Comorbid Autoimmune Conditions

| Condition | Screening Recommendation |
|-----------|--------------------------|
| **Autoimmune thyroid disease** (Hashimoto hypothyroidism, Graves hyperthyroidism) | TSH at diagnosis, then at repeated intervals if clinically indicated |
| **Celiac disease** | Screen if gastrointestinal symptoms, signs, or laboratory manifestations are present (diarrhea, malabsorption, abdominal pain, osteoporosis, vitamin/iron deficiencies) |
| **Pernicious anemia** (B12 deficiency) | Consider B12 levels (with reflex methylmalonic acid) in those with peripheral neuropathy or unexplained anemia |

## Other Associated Conditions

- Addison disease (primary adrenal insufficiency)
- Vitiligo
- Autoimmune liver disease
- Collagen vascular diseases
- Myasthenia gravis

## Genetic Syndromes

T1D may present within polyglandular autoimmune syndromes:
- **IPEX syndrome:** Caused by FOXP3 mutation; immune dysregulation, polyendocrinopathy, enteropathy, X-linked
- **AIRE gene mutation syndrome**

## Why It Matters

- Hypothyroidism worsens glycemic variability and lipid profile
- Celiac disease can cause malabsorption, weight loss, vitamin deficiencies, and osteoporosis — complicating T1D management
- B12 deficiency exacerbates peripheral neuropathy, which is already a T1D complication
