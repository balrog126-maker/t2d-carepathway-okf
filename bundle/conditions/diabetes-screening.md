## Who to Screen (Adults)

**Start at any age** if overweight/obese (BMI ≥25 kg/m², or ≥23 kg/m² in Asian ancestry) **plus ≥1 risk factor:**
- First-degree relative with diabetes
- High-risk race/ethnicity (African American, Latino, Native American, Asian American)
- History of cardiovascular disease
- Hypertension (≥130/80 mmHg or on antihypertensives)
- HDL <35 mg/dL and/or TG >250 mg/dL
- Polycystic ovary syndrome
- Physical inactivity
- Insulin-resistant conditions (severe obesity, acanthosis nigricans, MASLD)

**Screen all adults at age 35**, regardless of risk factors.

## Special Populations

| Population | Recommendation |
|-----------|----------------|
| Known prediabetes | Annually |
| Prior gestational diabetes | Every 1–3 years |
| HIV | FPG before starting ART, at switch, 3–6 months after; then annually (A1C less reliable in HIV) |
| Statins, thiazides, some HIV meds | Screen; these agents increase diabetes risk |
| Second-generation antipsychotics | Baseline, 12–16 weeks after initiation, then annually |
| Glucocorticoids (recurrent/long-term) | Monitor postprandial or random glucose (not fasting); glucocorticoids cause insulin resistance |
| Periodontal disease | Associated with dysglycemia; consider referral for diabetes screening |

## Children and Adolescents

Screen after onset of puberty or age 10 (whichever first) if:
- Overweight (≥85th percentile) or obese (≥95th percentile) **AND**
- ≥1 of: maternal GDM, first- or second-degree family history of T2D, high-risk race/ethnicity, insulin resistance signs (acanthosis nigricans, hypertension, dyslipidemia, PCOS, large- or small-for-gestational-age birth weight)

Repeat every 3 years minimum; more frequently if BMI increasing.

## Screening Interval (General)

- If normal: minimum every 3 years; sooner if symptoms develop or risk factors worsen
- Higher-risk individuals (values near cut points, ongoing risk medications): shorter intervals

## Screening Tools

- ADA Risk Test: awareness tool to guide whether formal diagnostic testing is warranted
- FPG, 2-h OGTT, or A1C all acceptable for screening; same tests used for diagnosis

## Setting

Screening ideally done in a health care setting to ensure follow-up. Community screening is generally not recommended unless an adequate referral system is established.
