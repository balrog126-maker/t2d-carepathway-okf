## Key Principle

No single eating pattern is recommended for all people with diabetes. The optimal pattern is one that achieves metabolic goals, is culturally appropriate, and can be sustained long-term.

## Validated Eating Patterns

### Mediterranean
- High in olive oil, fish, legumes, whole grains, vegetables, fruits
- Moderate wine (optional)
- **Benefits:** Reduced A1C, improved lipids, CVD risk reduction, supports T2D prevention in high-risk individuals (PREDIMED)

### DASH (Dietary Approaches to Stop Hypertension)
- Emphasizes fruits, vegetables, low-fat dairy, whole grains; limits sodium, red meat, added sugars
- **Benefits:** Blood pressure reduction, modest A1C improvement

### Plant-based / Vegan
- Eliminates or minimizes all animal products
- **Benefits:** Weight loss, A1C reduction, improved insulin sensitivity
- **Consideration:** Monitor B12 (supplement required); assess protein adequacy; can be carbohydrate-heavy if not planned carefully

### Low-Carbohydrate (<130g carbs/day or <26% calories)
- Reduces carbohydrate from all sources, prioritizing fat and protein
- **Benefits:** Significant short-term A1C and TG reduction; rapid postprandial glucose lowering; often requires insulin dose reduction
- **Considerations:** Variable long-term adherence; may raise LDL (saturated fat substitution); caution in CKD (high protein) and T1D (DKA risk if insulin underdosed)

### Very Low-Carbohydrate / Ketogenic (<50g carbs/day)
- Induces ketosis
- **Benefits:** Dramatic glucose reduction, often enables medication de-escalation in T2D
- **Risks:** Euglycemic DKA in T1D or with SGLT2 inhibitors; electrolyte disturbances; impractical for many; requires close monitoring

### Low Glycemic Index (Low-GI)
- Prioritizes foods with slower glucose absorption (legumes, barley, whole oats, non-starchy vegetables)
- **Benefits:** Modest A1C and TG reduction; useful as a practical framework

## Carbohydrate Management Tools

### Carbohydrate Counting
- Counts grams of total carbohydrate per meal
- Particularly important for patients on MDI insulin or insulin pump (ICR-based dosing)
- Basic: consistent daily carbohydrate intake per meal
- Advanced: match insulin-to-carbohydrate ratio (ICR) to meal carbs

### Plate Method
- 50% non-starchy vegetables / 25% lean protein / 25% quality carbohydrates
- Simple, effective for those not ready for carbohydrate counting
- Culturally adaptable

### Glycemic Index (GI) and Glycemic Load (GL)
- GI: how fast a food raises glucose relative to glucose reference
- GL = GI × grams carbohydrate / 100 (accounts for portion size)
- Low-GI foods: lentils, chickpeas, oats, whole-grain bread
- High-GI foods: white bread, white rice, cornflakes, sugary drinks

## Foods to Limit

- Sugar-sweetened beverages (soda, juice, sports drinks, energy drinks) — most impactful reduction
- Refined carbohydrates (white bread, white rice, processed cereals)
- Fried foods and trans fats
- Processed meats (associated with T2D risk)
- Alcohol in excess (calorie-dense, hypoglycemia risk with insulin/SU)

## Foods to Include

- Non-starchy vegetables (daily)
- Legumes and pulses (lentils, beans, chickpeas)
- Whole grains (oats, barley, farro, whole wheat)
- Nuts and seeds
- Fatty fish (2+ servings/week)
- Olive oil as primary fat
