## Weight Loss Goals in T2D

| Weight Reduction | Metabolic Benefit |
|-----------------|------------------|
| 5–7% body weight | Reduced progression from prediabetes to T2D; modest A1C improvement |
| ≥10% | Significant A1C, BP, and TG improvement; potential T2D remission |
| ≥15–20% | High probability of T2D remission; most likely with pharmacotherapy or surgery |

Target weight loss is individualized; sustained loss is more important than the method used.

## Lifestyle Interventions

### Intensive Lifestyle Intervention
- Structure: individualized eating plan + physical activity + behavioral change support
- Look AHEAD trial: 6% weight loss sustained at 8 years in T2D; significant A1C, BP, TG improvements
- Minimum: weekly contact for ≥6 months followed by monthly maintenance
- Delivered by: trained healthcare team, CDCES, RDN, or structured programs (e.g., National DPP for prediabetes)

### Caloric Restriction Strategies
- 500–750 kcal/day deficit → ~0.5–1 kg/week weight loss
- Any approach achieving sustained caloric deficit is valid: low-fat, low-carbohydrate, Mediterranean, or individualized
- **Total meal replacement** (very low calorie diet, 800–1000 kcal/day): rapid weight loss, high T2D remission rates at 1 year (DiRECT trial: 46% remission at 1 year with ~15 kg weight loss); requires intensive medical monitoring

## Weight Loss Pharmacotherapy

Indicated for BMI ≥30 kg/m² (or ≥27 + weight-related comorbidity) in those not meeting goals with lifestyle alone.

| Agent | Expected Weight Loss | Notes |
|-------|---------------------|-------|
| **Semaglutide (Wegovy)** 2.4 mg SC weekly | 12–15% body weight (STEP 1) | FDA-approved for obesity; also reduces MACE |
| **Tirzepatide (Zepbound)** SC weekly | 15–22% body weight (SURMOUNT-1) | Dual GIP/GLP-1 RA; most effective currently available |
| **Liraglutide (Saxenda)** 3 mg SC daily | 5–6% | Older GLP-1 RA; less potent |
| **Phentermine/topiramate** | 8–10% | CNS/sympathomimetic; not for CVD history |
| **Orlistat** | 3–5% | GI side effects; rarely used |
| **Bupropion/naltrexone** | 3–5% | CNS effects; minor BP elevation |

**Note:** GLP-1 RAs are preferred in T2D as they also improve glycemia, CVD risk, and kidney endpoints.

## Metabolic/Bariatric Surgery

Recommended for:
- BMI ≥40 kg/m² (any diabetes control)
- BMI 35–39.9 + inadequate glucose control despite optimal medical therapy
- May be considered for BMI 30–34.9 if difficult-to-control T2D (especially in Asian populations with lower BMI thresholds)

**Outcomes:**
- T2D remission at 1 year: 50–80% (Roux-en-Y), 30–60% (sleeve gastrectomy)
- A1C reduction: often dramatic
- Significant CV risk reduction (long-term observational data)
- Weight regain and diabetes relapse possible after 5–10 years

**Procedures:** Roux-en-Y gastric bypass > sleeve gastrectomy for glycemic remission.
**Monitoring post-surgery:** Annual nutrition labs (B12, D3, iron, folate, zinc, calcium), bone density, hypoglycemia screening.
