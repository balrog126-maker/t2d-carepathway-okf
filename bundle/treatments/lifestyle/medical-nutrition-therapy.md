## Definition

MNT is individualized nutrition counseling provided by a **Registered Dietitian Nutritionist (RDN)** as part of the diabetes care team. It differs from general nutrition advice in its specificity, clinical integration, and ongoing follow-up.

## Evidence

MNT reduces A1C by 0.5–2% in T2D and 1–2% in T1D. It improves lipids, blood pressure, and body weight independently of caloric restriction.

## Core Principles

- No single dietary pattern is universally superior; individualize based on preferences, culture, and metabolic goals
- Total carbohydrate amount (in grams) is the primary driver of postprandial glucose response
- Quality of carbohydrate (whole grain, fiber-rich, minimally processed) matters beyond quantity
- Reduce added sugars and refined grains; replace with whole foods
- Consistent carbohydrate intake at meals supports insulin dosing and glycemic predictability

## Recommended Eating Patterns

| Pattern | Key Features | Glycemic Benefit |
|---------|-------------|-----------------|
| **Mediterranean** | Olive oil, fish, legumes, vegetables, moderate wine | Reduces A1C, CVD risk, diabetes incidence |
| **DASH** | Low sodium, high potassium, whole grains, low-fat dairy | BP + A1C reduction |
| **Plant-based / Vegan** | Eliminate or minimize animal products | A1C and weight reduction; B12 supplementation needed |
| **Low-carbohydrate** | <26% energy or <130g carbs/day | Strong short-term A1C and TG reduction; long-term adherence variable |
| **Very low-carbohydrate / Ketogenic** | <50g carbs/day | May require insulin adjustment; monitor DKA risk in T1D |
| **Low glycemic index** | Emphasizes GI/GL | Modest A1C benefit |

## Specific Guidance

### Carbohydrates
- Choose non-starchy vegetables, legumes, whole grains, dairy over refined/processed carbs
- Avoid sugar-sweetened beverages, fruit juice, sweetened cereals
- Carbohydrate counting recommended for those on MDI or insulin pump

### Protein
- No benefit to very high protein for glycemic control
- Avoid high protein in those with CKD (may worsen proteinuria)

### Fats
- Replace saturated fats with unsaturated fats (olive oil, avocado, nuts)
- Avoid trans fats
- Omega-3 fatty acids (fatty fish, walnuts): cardioprotective

### Sodium
- <2,300 mg/day (consistent with general population)
- Lower targets for those with hypertension or CKD

### Alcohol
- Up to 1 drink/day (women) or 2 drinks/day (men) if choosing to drink
- Alcohol inhibits hepatic glucose production → hypoglycemia risk with insulin or SU
- Educate to eat a carbohydrate-containing meal before or with alcohol

### Non-nutritive Sweeteners (NNS)
- May help reduce caloric intake and postprandial glucose when substituted for sugar
- Evidence on long-term metabolic effects is mixed; use in moderation

## Plate Method (Practical Tool)

Divide plate into:
- 50% non-starchy vegetables
- 25% lean protein
- 25% quality carbohydrates (whole grains, legumes, starchy vegetables)

Useful for patients not ready for carbohydrate counting.
