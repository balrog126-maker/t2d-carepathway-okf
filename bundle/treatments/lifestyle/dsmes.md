## Definition

DSMES is a person-centered, collaborative process to facilitate the knowledge, decision-making skills, and abilities needed for effective diabetes self-care. It is the foundation of all diabetes management.

## Four Critical Times for DSMES Referral

| Time Point | Trigger |
|-----------|---------|
| **1. At diagnosis** | New diagnosis of any type of diabetes |
| **2. Annually thereafter** | Ongoing support and reinforcement |
| **3. Complicating factors arise** | New complication, change in regimen, major life change (job, pregnancy, older adult transition) |
| **4. Transition in care** | Change in provider, site of care, or health system |

## Evidence Base

DSMES consistently demonstrates:
- Reduced A1C (0.4–1.0% reduction in RCTs)
- Reduced hospitalizations and emergency care
- Improved medication adherence
- Improved quality of life, diabetes distress, and self-efficacy
- Significant cost savings (reduced complications, hospitalizations)

Longer contact time (>10 hours) produces greater glycemic improvements.

## Who Delivers DSMES

- **CDCES** (Certified Diabetes Care and Education Specialist) — primary credential
- Team-based: may include RDN, RN, pharmacist, behavioral health specialist, physical therapist
- Program must meet national standards (ADA/AADE recognized) for Medicare/Medicaid reimbursement

## Delivery Formats

- In-person individual or group
- Telehealth and video — equally effective; improves access in rural/underserved areas
- App-based or text-based support — emerging evidence
- Community health worker–delivered DSMES

## Reimbursement

- Medicare Part B: covers up to 10 hours in year 1 and 2 hours/year thereafter (DSMT)
- Medicaid: coverage varies by state
- Private insurance: increasingly covered, especially post-ACA
- ICD-10 codes and "recognized program" status required for billing

## Topics Covered

- Pathophysiology of diabetes
- Medication management (administration, side effects, adherence)
- Glucose monitoring and interpretation
- Medical nutrition therapy principles
- Physical activity
- Hypoglycemia prevention and treatment
- Sick-day management and hyperglycemic crisis prevention
- Foot care, eye care, dental care
- Psychosocial adjustment and distress management
- Smoking cessation
- Preconception and pregnancy planning
- Using healthcare and community resources
