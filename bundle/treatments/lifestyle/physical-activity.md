## Activity Recommendations

| Type | Target | Notes |
|------|--------|-------|
| **Aerobic** | ≥150 min/week moderate intensity OR ≥75 min/week vigorous | Brisk walking, cycling, swimming, dancing |
| **Resistance training** | 2–3 sessions/week on non-consecutive days | Free weights, machines, body weight; all major muscle groups |
| **Flexibility / balance** | 2–3×/week (especially older adults) | Yoga, stretching, tai chi; reduces fall risk |
| **Sedentary behavior** | Break sitting every 30 minutes | Light walking or resistance activity; independent benefit from TIR |

## Metabolic Benefits

- Lowers A1C 0.5–1%
- Increases insulin sensitivity (duration: up to 24–72 hours post-exercise)
- Reduces TG, raises HDL, lowers LDL
- Improves blood pressure
- Reduces weight and visceral adiposity
- Reduces cardiovascular event risk independent of glycemia

## Glycemic Effects During Exercise

| Exercise Type | Glucose Trend | Mechanism |
|---------------|--------------|-----------|
| Moderate aerobic (20–60 min) | Usually decreases | Increased glucose uptake without proportionate hepatic release |
| High-intensity aerobic (sprints, HIIT) | May transiently increase | Catecholamine surge → hepatic glucose release |
| Resistance training | Variable; often mild increase then late drop | Glucose uptake continues hours post-exercise |

## Insulin Adjustments for Exercise (T1D)

- Reduce prandial insulin 20–50% for planned aerobic exercise
- Consume 15–30g carbohydrate before aerobic activity if glucose <126 mg/dL
- CGM recommended during and up to 24h after vigorous exercise
- Nocturnal hypoglycemia risk is elevated after daytime exercise

## Pre-exercise Evaluation

- Cardiac evaluation recommended before vigorous exercise if: age >35, T2D >10 years, T1D >15 years, any end-organ damage
- Not needed for low-to-moderate intensity activity in otherwise healthy individuals
- Check glucose before exercise:
  - <90 mg/dL → eat carbs before starting
  - 90–250 mg/dL → exercise generally safe
  - >250 mg/dL → check ketones; if ketones present, delay exercise

## Special Considerations

| Complication | Precaution |
|-------------|-----------|
| Peripheral neuropathy | Wear appropriate footwear; inspect feet after exercise; avoid high-impact if severe |
| Autonomic neuropathy | Increased risk of orthostatic hypotension and cardiac arrhythmia; physician clearance |
| Proliferative retinopathy | Avoid vigorous, high-impact activity (risk of vitreous hemorrhage) |
| Nephropathy | No specific restrictions; exercise is beneficial |
| Hypertension | Avoid heavy isometric exercises (Valsalva); aerobic preferred |
