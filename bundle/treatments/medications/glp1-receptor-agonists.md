## Mechanism

GLP-1 receptor agonists activate the glucagon-like peptide-1 receptor, producing:
- Glucose-dependent insulin secretion (→ low hypoglycemia risk in monotherapy)
- Suppression of glucagon (reduces hepatic glucose output)
- Delayed gastric emptying (reduces postprandial glucose spikes)
- Central appetite suppression → reduced caloric intake and weight loss

## Cardiovascular Benefits (CV Outcomes Trials)

| Agent | Trial | MACE Benefit |
|-------|-------|-------------|
| Liraglutide | LEADER | 13% relative risk reduction |
| Semaglutide SC | SUSTAIN-6 | 26% RRR (MACE); secondary endpoints |
| Dulaglutide | REWIND | 12% RRR; T2D with moderate CV risk |
| Semaglutide oral | PIONEER-6 | Non-inferior; trend to benefit |
| Semaglutide SC (SELECT) | SELECT | 20% RRR MACE in obesity + CVD (no T2D required) |

**Tirzepatide** (SURPASS-CVOT): results anticipated; expected benefit based on mechanism and surrogate data.

## Kidney Benefits

- **Semaglutide** (FLOW trial): significant reduction in kidney composite endpoint (≥40% eGFR decline, ESKD, renal death) in T2D + CKD
- Preferred GLP-1 RA in T2D with CKD; no dose adjustment for eGFR if not on insulin

## Non-alcoholic Steatohepatitis (MASH/MASLD)

- **Semaglutide:** Reduces hepatic steatosis, lobular inflammation, fibrosis (NASH trial)
- Resmetirom (THR-β agonist) FDA-approved for MASH; GLP-1 RA complement
- **Preferred in T2D with MASLD** as part of the treatment algorithm

## GI Side Effects

Most common class effect:
- Nausea (20–40%), vomiting, diarrhea, constipation
- Usually occur at initiation and dose escalation; resolve within 4–8 weeks
- Mitigate by: slow dose escalation (monthly titration); small, low-fat meals; eat until no longer hungry

Rare but serious:
- **Pancreatitis:** Uncommon but class labeling; discontinue if pancreatitis suspected; not recommended if prior pancreatitis
- **Thyroid C-cell tumors:** Observed in rodent models; unknown human relevance; contraindicated in personal/family history of MEN2 or medullary thyroid carcinoma
- **Gastroparesis:** Delayed gastric emptying can worsen existing gastroparesis; use caution

## Perioperative and Procedural Considerations

- Hold GLP-1 RA (weekly agents) for 1 week before elective surgery requiring general anesthesia due to delayed gastric emptying → aspiration risk
- Hold daily agents 24h before

## Dual GIP/GLP-1 Receptor Agonist: Tirzepatide

- Activates both GIP (glucose-dependent insulinotropic polypeptide) and GLP-1 receptors
- Most effective A1C reduction and weight loss currently available in a non-surgical intervention
- FDA-approved for T2D (Mounjaro) and obesity (Zepbound)
- HF benefit: SUMMIT trial — significant improvement in HFpEF symptoms and 6-minute walk test in obesity
- Kidney outcomes trial ongoing
