## Mechanism

DPP-4 (dipeptidyl peptidase-4) is an enzyme that rapidly degrades endogenous GLP-1 and GIP (incretin hormones). DPP-4 inhibitors block this degradation, extending incretin activity:

- Increased GLP-1 → glucose-dependent insulin secretion from β-cells
- Increased GIP → augmented insulin release
- Reduced postprandial glucagon → less hepatic glucose output

Because the mechanism is **glucose-dependent**, these agents do not cause hypoglycemia in monotherapy.

## Common Agents

| Agent | Brand | Key Notes |
|-------|-------|-----------|
| **Sitagliptin** | Januvia | Most widely used; renal dose adjustment required |
| **Saxagliptin** | Onglyza | HF hospitalization signal — avoid in heart failure |
| **Alogliptin** | Nesina | Similar profile to sitagliptin |
| **Linagliptin** | Tradjenta | Hepatically cleared → no renal dose adjustment needed; preferred in CKD |

## Clinical Profile

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| A1C reduction | 0.5–0.8% |
| Weight effect | Neutral (no gain or loss) |
| Hypoglycemia risk (monotherapy) | None |
| Hypoglycemia risk (with insulin/SU) | Low; may require insulin or SU dose reduction |
| Route | Oral, once daily |

## Safety Considerations

### Heart Failure (Saxagliptin)
- SAVOR-TIMI 53 trial: saxagliptin associated with 27% increased risk of HF hospitalization
- **Avoid saxagliptin in patients with heart failure or at high HF risk**
- Sitagliptin, alogliptin, and linagliptin do not share this signal (TECOS, EXAMINE, CARMELINA trials)

### Pancreatitis
- Small increased signal in some trials; uncertain causality
- Discontinue if pancreatitis is suspected
- Use with caution in patients with prior pancreatitis history

### Nasopharyngitis and Upper Respiratory Infections
- Modest increased incidence (~1.5×); generally mild and self-limiting

### Renal Dosing
| Agent | Dose Adjustment |
|-------|----------------|
| Sitagliptin | 100 mg (eGFR ≥45), 50 mg (eGFR 30–44), 25 mg (eGFR <30 or dialysis) |
| Saxagliptin | 2.5 mg (eGFR <45) |
| Alogliptin | 12.5 mg (eGFR 30–59), 6.25 mg (eGFR <30 or dialysis) |
| Linagliptin | 5 mg (no renal adjustment needed) |

## Position in Algorithm

DPP-4 inhibitors are **second or third-line** add-ons, not first-choice agents. They occupy a specific niche:

- When GLP-1 RA cannot be used (cost, GI intolerance, injection refusal) and SGLT2i is not appropriate
- When **hypoglycemia risk is high** and weight neutrality is preferred over weight loss
- When **renal impairment limits other agents** and linagliptin is the pragmatic choice
- As combination tablets (e.g., sitagliptin/metformin) for pill-burden reduction

**Do not combine DPP-4 inhibitors with GLP-1 receptor agonists** — they act on the same incretin pathway with no additive benefit and added cost.

## Comparison vs. GLP-1 Receptor Agonists

| Feature | DPP-4 Inhibitor | GLP-1 RA |
|---------|----------------|----------|
| A1C reduction | 0.5–0.8% | 1.0–2.5% |
| Weight | Neutral | Loss (2–15+ kg) |
| MACE benefit | None proven | Yes (select agents) |
| Route | Oral | SC injection (most); oral semaglutide available |
| GI side effects | Minimal | Common (nausea, vomiting) |
| Cost | Moderate | High |
| Mechanism overlap | Yes — do not combine | — |
