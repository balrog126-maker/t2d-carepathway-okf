## Key Principle

All people with T1D require exogenous insulin for survival. Physiologic replacement consists of basal coverage (matching continuous hepatic glucose output) plus bolus coverage (matching carbohydrate intake and correcting hyperglycemia).

## Insulin Regimen Options

| Regimen | Description | Suitable For |
|---------|-------------|-------------|
| **MDI (Multiple Daily Injections)** | Basal insulin once or twice daily + prandial insulin with each meal | Standard for most T1D |
| **CSII (Insulin Pump)** | Continuous subcutaneous basal infusion + bolus on demand | T1D with variable schedules, frequent hypoglycemia, or pregnancy |
| **AID (Automated Insulin Delivery)** | CGM-connected pump with algorithm controlling basal in real time | T1D achieving best outcomes; dramatically reduces hypoglycemia and improves TIR |

## Dose Estimation

Starting total daily dose (TDD):
- **Adults:** 0.4–1.0 unit/kg/day (honeymoon phase: lower end; established T1D: 0.5–0.7 unit/kg/day typical)
- **Adolescents/puberty:** 0.8–1.5 unit/kg/day (insulin resistance)

**Basal:Bolus split:**
- ~50% of TDD as basal (covers fasting/overnight)
- ~50% of TDD as bolus (split across meals, carbohydrate-adjusted)

**Typical insulin-to-carbohydrate ratio (ICR):** 1 unit per 10–15g carbohydrate (varies widely; individualize).
**Correction factor (ISF):** 1800 ÷ TDD = expected glucose drop (mg/dL) per 1 unit.

## Insulin Analogs vs. Human Insulin

**Rapid-acting analogs** (lispro, aspart, glulisine) preferred over regular human insulin for bolus:
- Onset: 15 min vs. 30–60 min
- Peak: 1–2h vs. 2–4h
- Duration: 4h vs. 6–8h
- Allows dosing at meal start vs. 30 min before

**Ultra-rapid analogs** (Fiasp, Lyumjev):
- Onset: 5–10 min; used when meal timing is unpredictable

**Long-acting analogs** (glargine, detemir, degludec) preferred over NPH for basal:
- Flat activity profile → less overnight hypoglycemia
- Degludec (Tresiba): ultra-long action (42h), very flat profile, lowest hypoglycemia risk

## Automated Insulin Delivery (AID / Closed Loop)

Components: CGM + pump + control algorithm
- Adjusts basal every 5 minutes based on current and predicted glucose
- User still boluses for meals; system handles corrections and overnight
- Dramatically reduces TBR (<70 mg/dL) and improves TIR without increasing A1C
- Currently FDA-approved systems: Control-IQ (Tandem), MiniMed 780G, Omnipod 5, iLet (Beta Bionics)
- Recommended for all T1D with access and willingness; particularly impactful for patients with IAH

## Special Situations

### Exercise
- Reduce prandial insulin 20–50% for pre-planned aerobic activity
- Suspend or reduce AID basal 1–2h before exercise if using fixed 80% mode
- Monitor CGM during and 24h post-exercise for nocturnal lows

### Sick Days
- Never discontinue insulin; reduce dose minimally even if not eating
- Check ketones if glucose >240 mg/dL
- Supplement with correction doses every 2–4h

### Pregnancy
- Insulin requirements increase significantly in 2nd/3rd trimester
- Prefer MDI with carbohydrate counting or CSII+CGM
- A1C target <6.0–6.5% while avoiding hypoglycemia
