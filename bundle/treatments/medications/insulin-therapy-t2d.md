## When to Start Insulin in T2D

Insulin therapy should be initiated when:
- A1C >10% or glucose >300 mg/dL at presentation (often temporary — taper as possible)
- Symptomatic hyperglycemia (polyuria, polydipsia, weight loss)
- A1C ≥1.5% above goal despite optimized oral/non-insulin injectable regimen
- Impaired β-cell function (based on clinical course, C-peptide if needed)
- Pregnancy requiring tight glucose control not achievable with non-insulin agents

## Initiation Strategy: Basal-First

1. **Start basal insulin only** (preferred initial injectable approach)
   - Glargine 10 units/day OR 0.1–0.2 units/kg/day at bedtime or same time daily
   - Degludec or glargine U-300: lower hypoglycemia risk; preferred in high-risk patients
   - Detemir: requires twice-daily dosing in many patients

2. **Titration (Treat-to-Target):**
   - Increase dose by 2 units every 3 days until fasting glucose 80–130 mg/dL
   - If hypoglycemia: decrease by 4 units or 10%

3. **Continue non-insulin agents** where appropriate:
   - Metformin → continue
   - SGLT2i → continue (cardiorenal benefit)
   - GLP-1 RA → **continue or add** (combination reduces insulin doses 20–50%)
   - Discontinue SU and DPP-4i when adding insulin (overlapping mechanism or hypoglycemia risk)

## Injectable Intensification Algorithm

If basal insulin alone inadequate (A1C remains above goal despite optimized titration):

| Step | Option |
|------|--------|
| **Step 1** | Add GLP-1 RA (if not already) — reduces A1C and weight; preferred before adding bolus |
| **Step 2** | Add 1 prandial insulin dose (largest meal) |
| **Step 3** | Add 2nd prandial dose |
| **Step 4** | Full basal-bolus (3 meals + basal) OR switch to premixed insulin twice daily |

## Combination Products (Basal Insulin + GLP-1 RA)

| Product | Components | Benefit |
|---------|-----------|---------|
| iGlarLixi (Soliqua) | Glargine + lixisenatide | Single injection; weight-neutral to loss |
| IDegLira (Xultophy) | Degludec + liraglutide | Fewer hypoglycemic events vs. insulin alone |

These combinations cap the weight gain and hypoglycemia burden of insulin while providing GLP-1 benefits.

## Premixed Insulin

- Convenience for those not counting carbohydrates
- 70/30 (NPH/regular) or 75/25 (protamine lispro/lispro)
- Dosing: twice daily before breakfast and dinner
- Less flexible for variable meal schedules; higher hypoglycemia risk than basal-only

## Key Considerations

### Weight Gain
- Insulin promotes anabolic fat storage (~2–4 kg at initiation)
- Combine with GLP-1 RA or SGLT2i to minimize or reverse weight gain

### Hypoglycemia
- Basal insulin alone carries low hypoglycemia risk when properly titrated
- Bolus insulin significantly increases hypoglycemia risk; SMBG or CGM essential
- Avoid bedtime NPH — frequent nocturnal hypoglycemia

### De-intensification
- Reduce or discontinue insulin when: significant weight loss, diabetes remission after bariatric surgery, GLP-1 RA causing dramatic A1C improvement, or hypoglycemia recurrence
- Never abruptly discontinue in T1D or those with long-standing T2D with limited β-cell function
