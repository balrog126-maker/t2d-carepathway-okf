## Mechanism

SGLT2 (sodium-glucose cotransporter 2) inhibitors block glucose reabsorption in the proximal renal tubule, causing glycosuria (~60–80g glucose/day). Effects:
- Glucose lowering (glucose-independent of insulin)
- Osmotic diuresis → modest BP reduction (~3–5 mmHg systolic)
- Natriuresis → volume contraction → reduced cardiac preload
- Visceral fat loss (−1 to −3 kg)
- Ketogenesis stimulation (relevant for DKA risk in T1D)

| Agent | Trade Name | FDA Indications | A1C Reduction | HF Indication |
|-------|-----------|-----------------|--------------|--------------|
| **Empagliflozin** | Jardiance | T2D, HFrEF/HFpEF, CKD | 0.5–1.0% | All HF phenotypes |
| **Dapagliflozin** | Farxiga | T2D, HFrEF/HFpEF, CKD | 0.5–1.0% | All HF phenotypes |
| **Canagliflozin** | Invokana | T2D, CKD | 0.8–1.2% | Limited |
| **Ertugliflozin** | Steglatro | T2D | 0.5–0.7% | Limited |

| EMPEROR-Reduced | Empagliflozin | HFrEF −25% HF hospitalization |

## CKD Dosing Guidance

| eGFR | Empagliflozin/Dapagliflozin | Canagliflozin |
|------|---------------------------|--------------|
| ≥45 | Full dose for glycemia + organ protection | Full dose |
| 25–44 | Use for organ protection (glycemic effect reduced) | Limited use |
| 20–<25 | May continue for organ protection (dapagliflozin, empagliflozin data down to 20) | Contraindicated |
| <20 | Insufficient data; generally avoid | Contraindicated |

## Key Safety Considerations

### Genital Mycotic Infections
- 5–10× higher than placebo; more common in women
- Advise good hygiene; treat with topical antifungal; rarely requires discontinuation

### UTI
- Modest increased risk; rarely serious (Fournier's gangrene — rare but serious; seek evaluation for perineal pain/erythema)

### Euglycemic DKA
- Occurs in T1D (and rarely T2D) on SGLT2 inhibitors
- Glucose is often only 150–250 mg/dL → can be missed
- Mechanism: reduced insulin use + ketogenesis stimulation → ketone accumulation without expected hyperglycemia
- **Risk factors:** Fasting, carbohydrate restriction, alcohol, surgery, intercurrent illness, insulin omission
- Prevention: sick-day rules; hold SGLT2i ≥3 days before elective surgery; monitor ketones with illness

### Bone and Amputation (Canagliflozin)
- Canagliflozin: modest signal for lower extremity amputation (CANVAS); not seen with empagliflozin or dapagliflozin
- Use with caution in patients with peripheral arterial disease

### Volume Depletion / Hypotension
- More common in older adults and those on loop diuretics
- Hold temporarily during acute illness with poor intake / dehydration

## Sick-Day Rules for SGLT2 Users

Hold when:
- Acutely unwell with vomiting or inability to eat
- Fasting before procedure or surgery
- Hospitalized (high risk of dehydration)

Restart when eating/drinking normally.

Always check ketones (blood preferred) if symptomatic: nausea, vomiting, fatigue — even if glucose is <300 mg/dL.
