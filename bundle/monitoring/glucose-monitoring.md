## Blood Glucose Monitoring (BGM / SMBG)

Traditional fingerstick glucose monitoring remains appropriate when:
- CGM is not available or accessible
- Spot-checking specific moments (pre-meal, post-meal, bedtime, nocturnal)
- Calibrating CGM (for some devices)
- Confirming CGM alert readings before treatment decisions

**Frequency:** Individualized based on regimen:
- MDI insulin users: minimum 4×/day (before each meal and bedtime)
- Insulin pump users without CGM: minimum 4×/day
- Non-insulin T2D: as directed by treatment needs; routine SMBG has limited benefit in stable non-insulin users unless driving therapy adjustments

**Accuracy requirements:** FDA requires meters to be within 15% of reference at glucose >75 mg/dL and within 15 mg/dL at glucose ≤75 mg/dL.

## Continuous Glucose Monitoring (CGM)

### Types

| Type | Description | Examples |
|------|-------------|---------|
| Real-time CGM (rtCGM) | Continuous readings with alarms | Dexcom G7, Libre 3, Medtronic Guardian 4 |
| Intermittently scanned CGM (isCGM) | User must scan sensor to see glucose | FreeStyle Libre 2 |

### Who Should Use CGM

- All adults with T1D (strong recommendation)
- Adults with T2D on insulin (MDI or pump)
- Adults with T2D on non-insulin therapy + hypoglycemia episodes or A1C above goal
- Any patient where knowledge of glucose trends would change clinical decision-making

### CGM Benefits

- Reduces A1C in both T1D and T2D
- Reduces hypoglycemia (time below range)
- Improves TIR, especially when paired with AID systems
- Provides trend arrows to guide real-time dosing decisions
- Alerts for nocturnal hypoglycemia (otherwise often undetected)

### CGM Limitations

- Measures interstitial glucose (5–15 min lag vs. blood glucose during rapid changes)
- Less accurate at extreme glucose values (<40 or >400 mg/dL)
- Accuracy may be reduced by compression of sensor, excessive sweating, or off-label body sites
- Cost and insurance coverage remain barriers

## Alternative Biomarkers (When A1C Is Unreliable)

| Biomarker | Reflects | Turnaround | Limitation |
|-----------|---------|-----------|-----------|
| **Fructosamine** | Mean glucose ~2–3 weeks | Routine lab | Not standardized across labs; affected by hypoalbuminemia |
| **Glycated albumin** | Mean glucose ~2 weeks | Specialized | Affected by albumin turnover (nephrotic syndrome, liver disease) |
| **1,5-anhydroglucitol (1,5-AG)** | Hyperglycemic excursions (TAR) | Limited availability | Not approved for diagnosis |

Use fructosamine or glycated albumin in:
- Hemoglobin variants (HbS, HbC)
- Hemolytic anemia or chronic blood loss
- Pregnancy (T1D or T2D — A1C unreliable due to RBC turnover)
- HIV on ART

## AID Systems (Automated Insulin Delivery)

- CGM + pump + algorithm = closed-loop insulin delivery
- Adjust basal insulin every 5 min based on sensor glucose and predicted trajectory
- Dramatically reduce time below range and improve TIR in T1D
- Increasing use in T2D on insulin
