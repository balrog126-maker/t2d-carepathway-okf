## Classification System

| Level | Glucose Threshold | Definition |
|-------|------------------|-----------|
| **Level 1** (Alert Value) | <70 mg/dL (<3.9 mmol/L) | Alert value requiring treatment or medication adjustment; may or may not have symptoms |
| **Level 2** (Clinically Significant) | <54 mg/dL (<3.0 mmol/L) | Clinically significant; cognitive impairment possible; intervention required regardless of symptoms |
| **Level 3** (Severe) | No specific threshold | Severe cognitive/physical impairment requiring assistance; may include loss of consciousness, seizure |

## Acute Treatment: The 15-15 Rule

**For Level 1/2 (conscious, able to swallow):**
1. Consume 15–20g fast-acting carbohydrate (glucose tablets, 4 oz juice, regular soda, glucose gel)
2. Wait 15 minutes
3. Recheck glucose — repeat if still <70 mg/dL
4. Once glucose ≥70 mg/dL, eat a small snack if next meal is >1 hour away

**Avoid:** chocolate, candy bars, or high-fat snacks — fat slows glucose absorption.

**For Level 3 (unable to swallow or unconscious):**
- **Do NOT give oral carbohydrates** — aspiration risk
- Administer glucagon (see below) or call 911
- If IV access available: 25 mL of 50% dextrose IV or glucagon 1 mg IM/SC
- Position laterally until consciousness returns

## Glucagon Products

All patients using insulin and those at high hypoglycemia risk should have a glucagon kit prescribed.

| Product | Route | Dose | Notes |
|---------|-------|------|-------|
| Injectable glucagon (GlucaGen, Glucagon Emergency Kit) | IM or SC | 1 mg (adult) | Requires reconstitution |
| Nasal glucagon (Baqsimi) | Intranasal | 3 mg | No reconstitution; preferred for ease of use |
| Dasiglucagon (Zafgen) | SC | 0.6 mg | Liquid ready-to-use |

- Prescribe at least one to every patient on insulin
- Counsel family members and caregivers on use annually

## Nocturnal Hypoglycemia

- Often asymptomatic; detected primarily with CGM
- CGM low-glucose alerts at 70–80 mg/dL recommended for T1D and high-risk T2D
- AID systems (automated insulin delivery) with low-glucose suspend significantly reduce nocturnal events

## Prevention Strategies

| Strategy | Details |
|---------|---------|
| Reduce or discontinue precipitating agent | Lower insulin dose, switch from SU to low-risk agent |
| Structured BGMT or CGM | Identify patterns; adjust therapy proactively |
| Structured patient education | Meal timing, carbohydrate counting, sick day rules |
| Activity planning | Reduce prandial insulin 20–50% for anticipated exercise; consider pre-exercise carbohydrate |
| Bedtime snack guidance | Use only if glucose is trending down at bedtime |
| CGM alert settings | Low alarm at 70 mg/dL; urgent low alarm at 55 mg/dL |

## When to Reassess Glycemic Goals

Recurrent Level 2 or any Level 3 hypoglycemia → raise A1C target, review and loosen regimen, evaluate for IAH.
