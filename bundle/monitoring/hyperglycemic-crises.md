## Definitions

| Feature | DKA | HHS |
|---------|-----|-----|
| Typical population | T1D; also T2D, SGLT2 users | Older adults with T2D |
| Glucose threshold | Usually >250 mg/dL (may be lower in euDKA) | Usually >600 mg/dL |
| pH | <7.30 | Normal or near-normal |
| Bicarbonate | <15 mEq/L | Normal or slightly low |
| Ketones | Positive (moderate to large) | Absent or trace |
| Osmolality | Usually <320 mOsm/kg | >320 mOsm/kg |
| Mortality | ~1–5% | ~10–20% (higher in older adults) |

## Common Precipitants

- Infection or illness (most common — ~40% of cases)
- Missed or insufficient insulin doses
- New-onset T1D presenting for the first time
- Myocardial infarction, stroke, or major surgery
- SGLT2 inhibitors (euglycemic DKA — glucose may be only mildly elevated)
- Alcohol binge
- Psychological crisis or insulin omission

## SGLT2 Inhibitor-Associated Euglycemic DKA

- Occurs in T1D (and occasionally T2D) using SGLT2 inhibitors
- Glucose is often only 150–250 mg/dL — can be missed because euglycemia is not expected in DKA
- Check serum ketones or urine ketones in any SGLT2 user who is unwell, fasting, or perioperative
- **Hold SGLT2 inhibitors** at least 3–4 days before elective surgery or significant procedure

## Sick-Day Rules

Educate patients to:
1. **Never stop insulin** even if not eating (continue basal at minimum; reduce prandial if not eating)
2. Check glucose every 2–4 hours
3. Check ketones if glucose >240 mg/dL (urine or blood ketone meter)
4. Stay well hydrated with sugar-free fluids
5. Supplement electrolytes (potassium, sodium)
6. Contact provider or go to ED if:
   - Blood ketones >1.5 mmol/L or urine ketones moderate-to-large
   - Vomiting and unable to keep fluids down
   - Glucose >400 mg/dL and rising
   - Altered mental status

## SGLT2 Inhibitor Sick-Day Rules

- Hold SGLT2 inhibitor when acutely ill, fasting, or perioperative
- Resume only after eating and drinking normally
- Check ketones even if glucose appears acceptable

## Prevention of Recurrent DKA

| Intervention | Rationale |
|-------------|-----------|
| Diabetes education — sick-day rules | Most DKA is preventable with correct patient knowledge |
| Consistent insulin access (supply, cost barriers) | Insulin omission due to cost is a common cause |
| CGM with alarms | Identifies rising glucose trends early |
| Structured follow-up after any DKA episode | Identify root cause; adjust regimen |
| Closed-loop AID systems | Reduce incidence of hyperglycemia and DKA in T1D |
