## Definition

A1C measures the percentage of hemoglobin glycated at the beta-chain N-terminus (HbA1c). It approximates mean glucose over the preceding 2–3 months, weighted toward the most recent 4–6 weeks.

## Diagnostic Use

| A1C Value | Interpretation |
|-----------|---------------|
| <5.7% | Normal |
| 5.7–6.4% | Prediabetes |
| ≥6.5% | Diabetes (if confirmed) |

For diagnosis, A1C must be measured using an **NGSP-certified** assay **standardized to the DCCT reference**. Point-of-care A1C tests are NOT acceptable for diagnosis.

## A1C to Estimated Average Glucose (eAG) — ADAG Table

| A1C (%) | eAG (mg/dL) | eAG (mmol/L) |

| 5 | 97 | 5.4 |
| 6 | 126 | 7.0 |
| 7 | 154 | 8.6 |
| 8 | 183 | 10.2 |
| 9 | 212 | 11.8 |
| 10 | 240 | 13.4 |
| 11 | 269 | 14.9 |
| 12 | 298 | 16.5 |

Formula: eAG (mg/dL) = 28.7 × A1C − 46.7

## Frequency of Measurement

- Twice yearly: patients meeting glycemic goals with stable therapy
- Quarterly: patients not meeting goals or following therapy change

## Limitations — When A1C Is Unreliable

| Condition | Effect on A1C | Alternative |
|-----------|--------------|-------------|
| Hemolytic or iron-deficiency anemia | Falsely low | Fructosamine, glycated albumin |
| Hemoglobin variants (HbS, HbC, HbE, HbD) | Falsely low or high (assay-dependent) | Use HPLC assay; fructosamine |
| African/Mediterranean/Southeast Asian ancestry | More frequent Hb variants; verify assay compatibility | — |
| HIV infection | May underestimate | CGM or fructosamine preferred |
| Pregnancy (particularly 2nd/3rd trimester) | Falsely low due to RBC turnover | CGM-based TIR preferred |
| G6PD deficiency | Falsely low | Fructosamine |
| Recent blood transfusion | Falsely low | Fructosamine |
| Splenomegaly or hemodialysis | May alter | Fructosamine |

## A1C vs. CGM Metrics

A1C and TIR (time in range) are complementary, not interchangeable:
- A1C reflects average glucose; **does not capture hypoglycemia or glycemic variability**
- TIR better reflects day-to-day experience and is more strongly linked to patient-reported outcomes
- In T1D: A1C ≈7% corresponds roughly to TIR ≈70% (70–180 mg/dL)

## The "Glycemic Memory" Effect

Early improvement in A1C creates a legacy of reduced microvascular complications even if glycemic control subsequently worsens (UKPDS, DCCT/EDIC). Sustained A1C reduction is most impactful when started early in diabetes duration.
