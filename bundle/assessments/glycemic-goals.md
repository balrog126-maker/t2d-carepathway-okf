## Standard Glycemic Targets (Most Adults)

| Measure | Target |
|---------|--------|
| A1C | <7.0% |
| Preprandial glucose (BGM) | 80–130 mg/dL |
| Peak postprandial glucose (BGM) | <180 mg/dL |
| TIR (70–180 mg/dL) | >70% |
| TBR (<70 mg/dL) | <4% |
| TBR (<54 mg/dL) | <1% |
| TAR (>180 mg/dL) | <25% |

## Individualizing the A1C Target

The A1C goal is not one-size-fits-all. Use clinical judgment to determine whether tighter or more lenient goals are appropriate.

### Consider More Stringent Goals (A1C <6.5%)
- Short diabetes duration
- Long life expectancy
- No CVD, no significant comorbidities
- Low hypoglycemia risk (especially on CGM + AID or metformin)
- Strong personal motivation and resources

### Consider Less Stringent Goals (A1C <8% or higher)
- Severe or recurrent hypoglycemia or impaired hypoglycemia awareness
- Long diabetes duration with few resources
- Extensive comorbidities or limited life expectancy
- Frailty, dementia, or functional dependence
- Patient preference after shared decision-making

## Older Adults

| Category | A1C Target | Rationale |
|---------|-----------|-----------|
| Healthy, few comorbidities | <7.0–7.5% | Can benefit from tight control |
| Multiple comorbidities, moderate function | <8.0% | Reduce hypoglycemia risk |
| Frail, dementia, end-stage disease | <8.5% or no specific target | Symptom avoidance only |

- TIR target: >50% (70–180 mg/dL), TBR <1% (<70 mg/dL)
- Avoid targeting A1C so tightly that hypoglycemia occurs

## Pregnancy

| Condition | A1C | Glucose Targets |
|----------|-----|-----------------|
| Preexisting T1D or T2D | <6.0% if achievable without hypoglycemia | Preprandial: 70–95; postmeal 1h: <140; 2h: <120 mg/dL |
| Gestational diabetes | — | Same glucose targets; CGM TIR >90% in 63–140 mg/dL |

## Glycemic Memory / Legacy Effect

Early, sustained glycemic control in T1D (DCCT/EDIC) and T2D (UKPDS) significantly reduces long-term microvascular complications even if control later declines. This "legacy effect" supports aggressive early intervention.

## De-intensification

If A1C falls significantly below target and hypoglycemia is occurring:
- Reduce insulin doses, sulfonylureas, or other hypoglycemia-prone agents first
- Do not de-intensify in ways that prevent meeting minimal goals without patient input
- Reassess at every visit
