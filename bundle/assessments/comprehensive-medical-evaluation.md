## Purpose

The comprehensive evaluation confirms diagnosis, assesses glycemic status and treatment history, identifies complications and comorbidities, and enables person-centered care planning. Follow-up visits at minimum every 3–6 months, then at least annually.

## Initial Visit Components

### History
- Diabetes characteristics at onset (age, symptoms, type)
- Previous treatment plans and response
- Frequency/cause/severity of hospitalizations
- Family history: diabetes, autoimmune disorders
- Personal history: comorbidities (obesity, OSA, MASLD), cardiovascular disease, microvascular complications
- Hypoglycemia history: awareness, frequency, cause, timing
- Hemoglobinopathies or anemias
- Autoimmune disease history
- Last dental, foot, and dilated eye exams

### Behavioral Factors
- Physical activity, sleep, eating patterns, weight history
- Carbohydrate counting familiarity (relevant for T1D or MDI)
- Obstructive sleep apnea screening
- Tobacco, alcohol, and substance use

### Social Assessment
- Social support network; surrogate decision maker and advance care plan
- Social determinants of health: food security, housing, transportation, finances, community safety
- Daily routine and work/school schedule

### Medications
- Current plan; medication-taking behavior, rationing; intolerance or side effects
- Complementary/alternative medicine use
- Vaccination history

### Technology Use
- CGM, insulin pump, apps, connected pens
- Blood glucose monitoring data

## Physical Examination

| Element | Frequency |
|---------|-----------|
| Height, weight, BMI | Every visit |
| Blood pressure | Every visit |
| Orthostatic BP | Initial and annual |
| Skin exam (acanthosis, injection sites, lipodystrophy) | Every visit |
| Visual inspection of feet | Every visit (more frequent if high risk) |
| Fundoscopic exam (refer to ophthalmology) | Initial and annual |
| Thyroid palpation | Initial and annual |
| Comprehensive foot exam (temperature, vibration/pinprick, 10-g monofilament) | Initial and annual |
| Pedal pulses + ABI if PAD would change management | Initial and annual |
| Cognitive and functional performance | Initial and annual (if indicated) |
| Bone health (height loss, kyphosis) | Initial and annual |
| Depression, anxiety, diabetes distress, fear of hypoglycemia, disordered eating | Initial and annual |

## Laboratory Evaluation

| Test | Frequency |
|------|-----------|
| A1C | Every visit (if not within past 3 months) |
| Lipid profile | Initial, then as indicated |
| Liver function (FIB-4 index) | Initial and annual |
| Spot urine albumin-to-creatinine ratio (UACR) | Initial and annual |
| Serum creatinine and eGFR | Initial and annual |
| TSH | Initial and annual (T1D) |
| Celiac disease screen | Initial (T1D with suggestive symptoms) |
| Vitamin B12 | If on metformin >5 years |
| CBC with platelets | Initial and annual |
| Serum potassium | Initial and annual (if on ACE-i, ARB, or diuretic) |
| Calcium, vitamin D, phosphorous | As appropriate |

## Key Planning Activities

- Set glycemic goals (A1C, glucose, TIR targets)
- Set lipid and blood pressure goals
- Weight management and physical activity goals
- Lifestyle management plan (nutrition, DSMES)
- Pharmacologic therapy selection
- Referrals: ophthalmology, RDN, podiatry, dentistry, behavioral health, DSMES

## Person-Centered Communication

- Use neutral, nonjudgmental, strength-based language
- Avoid "noncompliant" or "nonadherent" — use person-first language
- Elicit individual preferences, values, barriers
- Assess health literacy and numeracy
