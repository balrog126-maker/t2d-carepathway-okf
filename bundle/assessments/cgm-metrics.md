## Core CGM Metrics (Ambulatory Glucose Profile)

| Metric | Definition | Units |
|--------|-----------|-------|
| **TIR** (Time In Range) | % time glucose 70–180 mg/dL | % per 24h |
| **TBR** (Time Below Range) | % time glucose <70 and <54 mg/dL | % per 24h |
| **TAR** (Time Above Range) | % time glucose >180 and >250 mg/dL | % per 24h |
| **GMI** (Glucose Management Indicator) | A1C estimate from mean glucose: GMI(%) = 3.31 + 0.02392 × mean glucose (mg/dL) | % |
| **CV** (Coefficient of Variation) | SD / Mean × 100; measure of glucose variability | % |
| **Mean glucose** | Average glucose from sensor readings | mg/dL or mmol/L |

## Population-Specific CGM Targets (Table 6.2)

| Population | TIR | TBR (<70) | TBR (<54) | TAR (>180) | TAR (>250) |
|-----------|-----|-----------|-----------|------------|------------|
| Most adults T1D/T2D | >70% | <4% | <1% | <25% | <5% |
| Older adults / High hypoglycemia risk | >50% | <1% | <1% | — | — |
| Pregnancy (T1D) | >70% in 63–140 mg/dL | <4% | <1% | <25% | <5% |
| Pregnancy (GDM/T2D) | >90% in 63–140 mg/dL | <4% | <1% | <10% | — |

## CV% (Glycemic Variability) Target

- **CV ≤36%** is the threshold for "stable" glycemia
- CV >36% indicates clinically meaningful variability → increased hypoglycemia risk
- CV is independent of mean glucose level

## GMI vs. A1C

- GMI estimates what A1C would be for a given mean CGM glucose
- GMI and laboratory A1C frequently disagree (often >0.5%) due to individual RBC lifespan, hemoglobin variants, or sensor accuracy
- If GMI and A1C diverge, investigate cause; do not average them

## Minimum Data Requirements

- At least **14 days** of sensor wear with **>70% data capture** to generate reliable AGP report
- Sensors should be calibrated or factory-calibrated per manufacturer instructions

## How to Read an AGP Report

1. Check overall TIR, TBR, TAR percentages
2. Identify overnight vs. daytime patterns
3. Identify peak postprandial excursions
4. Review CV% for variability
5. Note the 10th and 90th percentile traces vs. median

## Clinical Integration

- CGM-based TIR is used in addition to — not instead of — A1C
- TIR and TBR are most clinically actionable: high TBR → reduce insulin, adjust timing; low TIR → intensify overall control
- Sensor-based glucose alerts (high/low) are standard in AID systems
